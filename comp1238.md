## Week 1
- Writing and record-keeping existed thousands of years before computers.
- Many computer terms come from printing presses and typewriters.

## Week 2
- All data structures and formats, no matter how complex, are represented as sequences of bytes.
- A bit is the tiniest piece of information in computing and can hold one of two values (0/1, true/false, on/off). 

## Week 3
- Text files have structure, such as lines, whitespace, indentation, and paragraphs. This is to help organize and interpret the text.
- Browsers are extremely complex projects, kept consistent by strict standards set by W3C and other organizations.

## Week 4
- IDEs are editors that include many other tools and are often language-specific.
- 3 types of keyswitches are linear, tactile, and clicky.

## Week 5
- The CLI is still used today because it provides precise control, automation, efficiency, remote capability, and access to functionality that may not exist in a GUI.
- Unix philosophy: make each program do one thing well, write programs to work together, and handle text streams as a universal interface.
